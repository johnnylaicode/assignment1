THE LIONS

The lion is the second largest member of the cat family after the tiger. Lions live in parts of Africa and India. Lions are the only cats that live in groups called prides. A pride is made up of related lionesses, their cubs, and one or two adult males. Prides average 15 members. Lionesses usually do most of the pride’s hunting, mainly at night. Lions are carnivorous, meaning they survive by eating other animals. They eat almost anything including antelope, buffalo, giraffes, crocodiles, baby elephants, lizards and bugs. They have also been known to sometimes steal food from other big cats. Male lions spend their time guarding their territory and their cubs. They maintain the boundaries of their territory, which can be as large as 260 sq. km (100 sq. mi.), by roaring, marking it with urine, and chasing off intruders. Their thick manes, a unique trait to male lions, protect their necks when they fight with challengers. In the wild, lions live for an average of 12 years and up to 16 years. They live up to 25 years in captivity. Though lions are often called the ‘king of the jungle’, most lions actually live in the savannah or grasslands. There is only one population of wild forest-dwelling lions left in Gir Forest National Park, India.
